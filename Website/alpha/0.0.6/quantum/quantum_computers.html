Quantum Computation

Quantum computers are the most famous and powerful quantum technology yet considered. Building such a machine is massively challenging, however. An overview of what quantum computers would be capable of is given in this video.

All quantum technology can be traced to its origin in the 1980s, when Richard Feynmann suggested that to simulate the quantum nature of chemistry would require a quantum simulation device. This idea grew into the idea of a Quantum Computer, capable of generic tasks and which offered solutions to problems normal computers can't achieve. The field of quantum computing was advanced in the 90's by the realisation that a quantum algorithm could be used to factorise large numbers (Shor's algorithm). This cast doubt on modern methods of encryption, and indicated that quantum computers may be useful tools for a number of tasks.
